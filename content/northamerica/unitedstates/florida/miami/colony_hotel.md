The Colony Hotel at 736 Ocean Drive is, by general consent, the most photographed building in Miami Beach — and deservedly so. Henry Hohauser completed it in 1935, making it one of the first truly streamlined structures on the strip. Only three stories tall, it punches far above its height: banded teal stripes separate floors of large glass windows, and at the center, an inverted-T neon marquee spells out COLONY in letters that glow baby blue at night. Hohauser's original drawings note the neon sign as a "unique design" — an innovation at the time, when most hotels still relied on painted wooden boards. The Colony was also the first Ocean Drive building to use steel corner windows, which allowed the continuous glass curves that define the streamline style. Stand across the street after dark and the whole facade dissolves into reflection and neon: it is impossible to look at it without understanding why Barbara Capitman fought so hard to keep every last building on this block.
