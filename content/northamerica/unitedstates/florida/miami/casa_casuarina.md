Casa Casuarina at 1116 Ocean Drive was built in 1930 in the Mediterranean Revival style for Alden Freeman, a Standard Oil heir who modeled it loosely on the Alcazar in Santo Domingo. Gianni Versace bought it in 1992 and spent three years expanding it — adding a pool tiled with 24-karat gold mosaic, doubling the property onto the lot behind it, filling every surface with hand-painted murals. On the morning of July 15, 1997, the designer was returning from his daily walk to the News Cafe when Andrew Cunanan shot him twice on these very front steps. The murder convulsed the city, landed on every front page in the world, and became one of those moments permanently attached to a specific address. The building is now a hotel, and the mosaic pool is available for a fee. The wrought-iron gate where Versace fell is unchanged. Whatever you think of the circumstances, the architecture is extraordinary: stand at the gate and look at the entrance loggia — the carved stone, the bougainvillea spilling over the walls — and you see exactly why he wanted to live here.
