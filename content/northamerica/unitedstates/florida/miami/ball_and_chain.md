Ball & Chain opened in 1935 at 1513 SW 8th Street, and by the 1940s it was one of the few clubs in Miami where Black performers could play — Billie Holiday and Count Basie both played the stage here during the segregation era, in a city that had strict rules about where Black musicians could perform and where they could sleep. The club closed in 1957 as the neighborhood shifted, and the building sat through decades of different incarnations before being restored and reopened in 2014 as a salsa and Latin jazz venue. The restored space keeps the original neon sign and much of the interior character — tile floors, pressed-tin ceilings, a long bar facing a small stage. On weekend nights the music spills out onto the pavement and you can hear the brass from half a block away. It is one of the few places in Little Havana where the neighborhood's pre-exile past and its Cuban present exist in the same room.
