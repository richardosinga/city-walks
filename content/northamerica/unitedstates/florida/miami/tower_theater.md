The Tower Theater opened in 1926 as a neighborhood cinema for a Jewish and working-class Southern crowd — the kind of house where serials ran on Saturdays and families came by streetcar. By the 1980s, as the surrounding streets had become the cultural capital of Cuban exile Miami, it was showing Spanish-language films to audiences for whom Hollywood blockbusters were simply too far a cultural distance. The building itself is a stripped-down Art Deco tower in cream and terracotta, its vertical sign still visible from blocks away down Calle Ocho. After a long closure it was restored and reopened in 2002, operated by Miami Dade College as a venue for film, performance, and community events. It functions now as the unofficial gateway to the neighbourhood — the first thing you see when you walk west from downtown into Little Havana, and a reminder that this street has always been a place where stories are told in more than one language.
