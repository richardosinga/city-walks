The museum at 1821 SW 9th Street is run by Brigade 2506 — the veterans' association of the Cuban exile force that landed at the Bay of Pigs on April 17, 1961, and was defeated within three days. Of the roughly 1,400 men who landed, 114 were killed and 1,202 captured. The survivors were ransomed back to the United States in December 1962 in exchange for food and medicine worth $53 million. Most of those men settled in Miami. The museum is small and personal in the way that veterans' museums tend to be: photographs, weapons, uniforms, dog tags, maps of the landing sites. What it communicates above all is the shape of the betrayal — the US government trained and equipped the brigade, then withdrew the air cover at the last moment, leaving the men on the beach without the means to fight back. John F. Kennedy accepted responsibility publicly. The men of Brigade 2506 never entirely forgave him. Their flag, carried ashore at Girón and brought back to Miami, hangs inside.
