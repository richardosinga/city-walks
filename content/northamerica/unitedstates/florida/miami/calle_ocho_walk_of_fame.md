Embedded in the pavement of SW 8th Street at intervals between 14th and 16th Avenues, the Walk of Fame stars honor Latin musicians, entertainers, and cultural figures who shaped the exile generation and its descendants. Celia Cruz is here — the Cuban singer who left Havana in 1960 on a brief tour and never returned, spending the next four decades performing with a ferocity that seemed to carry the weight of everything she had left behind. Gloria Estefan is here, as is the bandleader Tito Puente and the actor Desi Arnaz, who was born in Santiago de Cuba and became, in his own way, one of the first Cuban faces in American popular culture. The stars are pink terrazzo inlaid with brass lettering — modest in size, easy to miss if you are not looking down. They are best read slowly, as a list of what the exile community chose to celebrate about itself: music above everything, and the idea that art survived the crossing even when everything else was lost.
