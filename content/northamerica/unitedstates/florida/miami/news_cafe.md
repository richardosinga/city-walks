Mark Soyka opened the News Cafe at 804 Ocean Drive in 1988, at a time when this stretch of oceanfront was still considered a backwater. The concept was simple: a cafe, bar, and international newsstand, open 24 hours, seven days a week, with tables spilling out onto the sidewalk so you could read Le Monde while watching the Atlantic catch the morning light. It worked. Within a few years the place was serving four to five thousand customers a day, cycling through multiple entirely different crowds — locals with espresso at dawn, tourist families at noon, models and photographers in the afternoon, clubgoers at 3 a.m. looking for something to eat. The most faithful regular was Gianni Versace, who walked over each morning from his mansion at 1116 Ocean Drive to collect his newspapers. On July 15, 1997, he had done exactly that — and was shot dead on his front steps minutes after returning. The News Cafe closed in 2021, but reopened in 2023. Order a coffee and sit outside: the scene itself has not changed much.
