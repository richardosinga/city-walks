Henry Hohauser designed the Park Central in 1937, and the seven-story result is widely considered the crown of Ocean Drive. Known locally as the "Blue Jewel," it rises higher than almost anything else on the strip, its stucco facade layered with eyebrow ledges, porthole windows, and a subtle nautical geometry that recalls an ocean liner docked between the palms. In the 1940s the lobby welcomed Clark Gable and Rita Hayworth; during World War II the military requisitioned the building as officer billets. The eyebrow ledges — horizontal concrete fins cantilevered over each window — were a practical invention: they shaded the glass from the subtropical sun before air conditioning existed. Look up at them and you see the whole logic of South Beach Deco compressed into a single detail: beauty arrived at through necessity.
