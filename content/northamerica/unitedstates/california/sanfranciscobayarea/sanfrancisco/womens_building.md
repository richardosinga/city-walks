The building itself is a converted meeting hall from the 1910s at 3543 18th Street. What makes it impossible to miss is MaestraPeace, the five-storey mural that covers every exterior surface and is one of the largest and most visited murals in the United States.

MaestraPeace was painted in 1994 by seven Bay Area muralists: Juana Alicia, Miranda Bergman, Edythe Boone, Susan Kelk Cervantes, Meera Desai, Yvonne Littleton, and Irene Perez. The title — a bilingual pun on "masterpiece" and "maestra" (woman teacher) — announces its project. The mural covers the building in a dense iconographic program celebrating women across history and cultures, incorporating more than 600 women's names into the design. The 18th Street facade is dominated by a monumental nude pregnant goddess with butterfly wings holding up the sun; the Lapidge Street side is capped by a portrait of Rigoberta Menchú, the Guatemalan indigenous rights activist who won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1992.

The Women's Building has operated as a women's community centre since 1979, when the San Francisco Women's Center purchased it. It runs programmes in health, legal services, and community organising, and its meeting rooms have hosted most of the significant feminist and social justice organisations that have passed through the Bay Area over the past four decades.
