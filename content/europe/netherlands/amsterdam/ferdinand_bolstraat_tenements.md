The main spine of De Pijp, running south from Marie Heinekenplein to Ceintuurbaan. The four-storey brick tenements lining its length were built between 1870 and 1890 by speculative developers — a type known in Dutch as *revolutiebouw* (revolution-building), named not for politics but for the speed and novelty of the construction method. The facades are typically two windows wide, with ready-made ornamental details applied to the brickwork — pilasters, cornices, and decorative panels — to suggest respectability on a tight budget. In September 1876, two newly completed houses on this street collapsed during construction, pulling a third down with them.
