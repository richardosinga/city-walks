A small park at the heart of the Spaarndammerbuurt, the neighbourhood built almost entirely between 1913 and 1923 by the Amsterdam School's principal architects for the socialist housing cooperative De Arbeiderswoning. The surrounding streets are an outdoor museum of the movement at its most ambitious: each block distinct, the rooflines constantly varying, the brickwork treated as a sculptural material rather than a structural necessity. The building at the corner of Hembrugstraat and Zaanstraat, designed by Michel de Klerk in 1917, has a roofline that rises in a long convex curve to a prow-like corner — a deliberately nautical profile for a city built on water.
