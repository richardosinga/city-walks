The longest and busiest street market in the Netherlands, running the full 300-metre length of Albert Cuypstraat six days a week. Founded informally in the late 19th century and regularised by the city in 1905, the market sells everything from fresh stroopwafels and raw herring to fabric, electronics, and spices from Suriname, Turkey, and Morocco. A bronze statue of folk singer André Hazes — who was discovered singing here as a child in 1959 — stands near the western entrance.
