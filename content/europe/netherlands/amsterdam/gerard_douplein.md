A small pedestrian square where Gerard Doustraat crosses Albert Cuypstraat, roughly in the middle of the market. The square is named for Gerrit Dou (1613–1675), a Dutch Golden Age painter who studied under Rembrandt in Leiden before developing his own style of small, extraordinarily detailed interiors. Like most of De Pijp's streets, Gerard Doustraat was named for a painter not out of particular admiration but because the neighbourhood was being built so fast in the 1870s that the city simply worked through a list. The square widens the market street and gives a natural pause — the stalls press close on both sides, and looking north along Gerard Doustraat you can see the four-storey tenements that give De Pijp its character.
