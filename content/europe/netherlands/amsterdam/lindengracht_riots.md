A street with two riots. In 1886, police cut the rope at an illegal eel-pulling contest here and triggered three days of fighting that left 26 dead — the Palingoproer, or Eel Riots. In 1934, welfare cuts brought the Jordaan back to the barricades: residents tore up cobblestones, lifted bridges to seal off the neighbourhood, and on 5 July a 26-year-old metalworker named Jan Gerressen was shot through the forehead on this street. Five confirmed dead, possibly many more. There is no plaque.
