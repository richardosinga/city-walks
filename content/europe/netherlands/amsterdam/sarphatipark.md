A small Victorian park at the eastern edge of De Pijp, designed in 1885 and named for Samuel Sarphati (1813–1866), a Jewish physician who transformed 19th-century Amsterdam through public health initiatives, a bread factory for the poor, and grand building projects including the Amstel Hotel. During the German occupation, the park was renamed Bollandpark in 1942 because Sarphati was Jewish; the original name was restored in 1945. A monument to Sarphati stands at the park's centre.
