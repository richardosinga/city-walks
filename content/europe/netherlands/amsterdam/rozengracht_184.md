Rembrandt van Rijn's last address. He moved here in 1658 after bankruptcy forced the sale of his grand house on the Jodenbreestraat, and he lived here until his death on 4 October 1669. His partner Hendrickje Stoffels died here in 1663; his son Titus in 1668. When Rembrandt died, a notary inventoried the estate: 50 items, including 26 unfinished paintings. He was buried in a rented pauper's grave in the Westerkerk. The building currently houses a tattoo studio.
