Three adjoining houses at Ceintuurbaan 251–255, built in 1884 to a design by A.C. Boerma and listed as a rijksmonument since 1984. Where the surrounding De Pijp tenements apply their ornament from a catalogue, this building applies it in almost every available style at once: lancet windows and gargoyles from the Gothic Revival, kruiskozijn cross-frames and stone banding from the Dutch Renaissance, and carved wooden bay windows with the overhanging eaves of a Swiss chalet. At the top of each bay window and on the gable peaks, small gnome figures appear to throw a ball between them — thought to be a play on the client's surname, Van Ballegooijen, meaning approximately "to throw a ball." The cumulative effect is eclectic to the point of being cheerful, which makes it one of the most immediately recognisable buildings on Ceintuurbaan.
