A modest square on the boundary between Oud-West and the Westerpark area, surrounded by some of the earliest surviving Amsterdam School housing — built between 1909 and 1914, before the movement found its full confidence. The buildings around the square, by Piet Kramer and collaborators, are experiments in what a housing block could do beyond the *revolutiebouw* formula: decorative panels in contrasting brickwork, corner treatments that break the roofline, ornamental ironwork that is integral to the façade rather than applied to it. The square is worth comparing to De Dageraad in De Pijp — earlier, quieter, less sure of its own arguments, but already making them.
