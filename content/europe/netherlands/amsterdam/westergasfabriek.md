The former municipal gasworks, built in stages from 1883 and operating until 1992. The main purifier building is a steel-and-glass structure whose Victorian industrial logic is entirely exposed — closer in character to an English railway terminus than to any contemporary Dutch model. The gasometer frame, a skeletal steel cylinder that once held the gas holder, still stands at the northern edge of the site. The conversion of the complex into cultural venues, restaurants, and park (completed in stages from 2003) was handled with unusual restraint: the industrial character of the buildings was preserved, additions are clearly distinguishable from the original fabric, and the park is a serious park. One of the better examples of post-industrial regeneration in the Netherlands.
