A housing complex built between 1919 and 1923 for the socialist housing association De Dageraad (The Dawn), designed by Michel de Klerk and Piet Kramer — the two leading architects of the Amsterdam School. The complex wraps around Pieter Lodewijk Takstraat and Burgemeester Tellegenstraat, incorporating 294 dwellings and six shops. Where the older De Pijp tenements use applied ornament as a thin veneer over cheap construction, De Dageraad integrates decoration into the structure itself: the brickwork is laid in multiple colours, windows are set asymmetrically, rooflines swell and dip, and corner balconies curve outward. It was a deliberate architectural argument against the speculative housing that surrounded it. A small museum at Burgemeester Tellegenstraat 128 (Museum de Dageraad) is open to the public.
