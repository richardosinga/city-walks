---
title: "De Drie Hendricken"
type: poi
latitude: 52.374285
longitude: 4.879697
---

Three nearly-identical stepped-gable houses at Bloemgracht 87, 89, and 91, built in 1642 and named after their builder, Hendrick Roelofsz. Each carries a carved gable stone above the door: a townsman, a farmer, and a sailor — the three pillars of the Dutch economy. Willem Blaeu, the cartographer who mapped much of the known world, had his workshop on this canal from 1635. At least 14 sugar refineries operated here in the Golden Age.
