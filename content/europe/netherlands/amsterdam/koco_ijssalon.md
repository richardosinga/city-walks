The former site of Koco, an ice cream parlour run by German-Jewish refugees Ernst Cahn and Alfred Kohn at Van Woustraat 149. After repeated attacks by Nazi sympathisers, regulars secretly rigged an industrial ammonia canister at the entrance. When German police raided on 19 February 1941, Cahn was arrested and tortured for the name of the person who built it. He never gave it up. On 3 March 1941 he was shot — the first person executed in the Netherlands under Nazi occupation. The crackdown that followed directly triggered the February Strike of 25–26 February 1941, in which 300,000 Amsterdam workers stopped work: the only mass popular strike against Jewish persecution in occupied Europe. A memorial plaque marks the site.
