A former municipal tram depot completed in 1902 to designs by the engineer W.C. Metzelaar, converted into a cultural complex in 2014. The main hall — barrel-vaulted brick and cast-iron, built to hold as many trams as possible under a single roof — is now a covered food and flea market on weekends, with a cinema, hotel, library, and co-working space occupying the surrounding buildings. The conversion preserved the industrial structure almost entirely; the additions are light and reversible. On market days the cast-iron columns carry market lights, and the scale of the Victorian industrial space is more visible than when the hall was full of trams.
