The western end of Brouwersgracht offers the fullest gable survey in Amsterdam: neck gables (halsgevel), spout gables (tuitgevel), and bell gables (klokgevel) stand side by side along the waterfront. Many of the larger buildings here were 17th-century pakhuizen — warehouses used by brewers and merchants whose trade gave the canal its name. The spout gable, with its plain triangular or rounded top and no classical ornament, marks a working building: functional, wide, built for loading rather than display. The neck gable alongside it, with its curved flanking volutes and sandstone pilasters, announces a merchant who wanted the world to know the difference.
