A 17th-century hofje built on top of a plague cemetery. The site was a medieval Carthusian monastery until the iconoclasm of 1566, then a plague burial ground from 1602. In 1650, city architect Daniël Stalpaert — who also designed the Royal Palace on Dam Square — built 91 small dwellings here for the widows of sailors and merchants. The courtyard is open daily 10am–5pm.
