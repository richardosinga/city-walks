Lindengracht is one of the wider streets in the northern Jordaan, and a good place to read the ordinary Jordaan house type in bulk: four storeys, narrow brickwork, a hoisting beam (hijsbalk) projecting from the gable peak. The hijsbalk is the key detail — Amsterdam houses were too narrow for interior staircases to carry large goods, so furniture, barrels, and sacks of grain went up the outside, hoisted by rope through the widened upper-floor window. Nearly every Jordaan house has one. The facades are plain by design: these were workers' houses, not merchant houses, and the ornament budget went to the gable top rather than the entrance.
