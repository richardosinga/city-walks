A quiet canal running along the southern edge of the older part of De Pijp, named for the 17th-century Dutch landscape painter Jacob van Ruisdael. Most of De Pijp's streets are named for Dutch painters — Rembrandt, Frans Hals, Jan Steen — which gives the neighbourhood its character of a city within a city. The canal-side houses here are among the most attractive in the area, their reflections in the water a reminder that De Pijp, for all its density, was built on Amsterdam's reclaimed land.
