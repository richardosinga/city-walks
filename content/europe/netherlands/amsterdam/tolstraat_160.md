---
title: "Tolstraat 160"
type: poi
latitude: 52.352628
longitude: 4.906321

A former meeting hall built in 1925–1926 for the Theosophical Society, designed by architects J.A. Brinkman and L.C. van der Vlugt in the style of *Het Nieuwe Bouwen* (New Objectivity). The building has a quarter-circle floor plan — the two straight side facades converge at a right angle, meeting at a pointed stage end, while the curved front faces the street. Each side facade is interrupted by a large diagonal window section, subdivided into narrow vertical panes set at an angle in the wall plane. The form follows directly from the function: the hall held around 750 people, all looking toward the point. Today the building serves as a branch of the Amsterdam public library (OBA). It is a rijksmonument, and stands in deliberate contrast to the Amsterdam School expressionism found only a few streets away: where De Dageraad curves and ornaments, Tolstraat 160 strips back and angles.
