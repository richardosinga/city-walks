One of the cross-streets typical of De Pijp's interior, named for the painter Jan Steen (1626–1679). The buildings here are among the most legible examples of the neighbourhood's standard ornamental vocabulary: narrow plots, four storeys, brick facades with applied pilasters dividing the bays, and decorative cornices running between the window levels. These details were not designed individually — they came from catalogues, cast in terracotta or pressed from brick, and applied by builders working to a budget. The effect is an ornament that signals respectability without the cost of employing an architect.
