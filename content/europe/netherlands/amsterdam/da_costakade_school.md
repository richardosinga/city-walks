A canal forming the western edge of Oud-West proper, where the housing along the west bank documents the development of the Amsterdam School style from its earliest tentative experiments (c.1908) to its fully confident mid-period (c.1920). Building by building, moving north, you can watch the rooflines become more fluid, the façade surfaces more sculptural, the use of decorative brick increasingly confident. The blocks here were expensive to build for their working-class occupants and caused recurring arguments with the city housing authority about cost. The counterargument — that people who live in places designed with intention live better — was Michel de Klerk's position, and the canal is a sustained illustration of it.
